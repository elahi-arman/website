The Cost of Cutting Class

I can empathize coming back to school is tough, especially with COVID-19 looming over our heads, TikTok occupying most of our heads, and the ever-present dread that comes with the end of school seemingly on the horizon. When I was in my first year of university, I often skipped class.

There was one particular class that took attendance on a sheet of paper. I suckered one of my friends into going and writing my name down to make it seem like I was there. I attended maybe 40% of that class and managed to pass. But somewhere in that quarter, my brother sat me down for a math exercise. He asked

"Do you know how much cutting class costs?"

I didn't know. So we sat together and did the math

  • Assume tuition is $50,000 (whether from your pocket or someone else's) per year.
  • Each school year is made of 3 quarters.
  • Each quarter, a full-time student takes 20 units.
  • For every 4 unit class, you're in class for about 30 hours (3 hours / per week over 10 weeks).
  • That means each class costs about $100.

Sometimes, you'll find that it was worth it, but remember your full-time job is to be a student. That means learn and absorb knowledge at least 30 hours per week.

Once you get to the work place, you won't have that luxury. You might get 2 hours per week on the job if you're lucky. If you're unlucky, that means you have a full-time 40+ hours per week job and a side gig where you could be burnt out.

So, if you're thinking of cutting class or worse, not paying attention, I hope you've got $100 to spare.
